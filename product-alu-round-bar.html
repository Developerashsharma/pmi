Understanding Aluminum Temper Designations:

T6 Temper (Solution Heat Treated & Artificially Aged): Most common temper for 6061, 6082, 2014, 7075. Material is solution heat treated at 530°C, quenched, then aged at 175°C to achieve maximum strength and hardness. Provides best combination of strength, hardness, and dimensional stability.

T4 Temper (Solution Heat Treated & Naturally Aged): Lower strength than T6 but better formability. Used when subsequent forming operations are required.

O Temper (Annealed): Softest condition with maximum ductility. Used for forming, bending, and when strength is not critical.

T651 Temper: T6 temper with stress relief by stretching (1-3%). Reduces residual stresses, improves machining stability, prevents warping in precision parts.

Extruded vs. Cold Drawn Aluminum Bars:

Extruded Aluminum Bars: Manufactured by pushing heated aluminum billet through a die. More economical, available in larger diameters (up to 500mm), suitable for general applications. Surface finish: mill finish with slight extrusion lines.

Cold Drawn Aluminum Bars: Extruded bars further processed by cold drawing through precision dies at room temperature. Superior surface finish (bright, smooth), tighter dimensional tolerances (+/- 0.05mm), improved mechanical properties. Ideal for precision machining, turned parts, components requiring excellent surface finish. Available up to 100mm diameter typically.

Complete Aluminum Alloy Grades & Selection Guide

Why 6061-T6 is the Industry Standard

Designation: 6061-T6 (UNS A96061, EN AW-6061)
Primary Alloying Elements: Magnesium (0.8-1.2%) and Silicon (0.4-0.8%)
Strength Level: Medium-high strength (310 MPa tensile, 276 MPa yield)

Key Characteristics:
  • Excellent Machinability: 90% rating makes it ideal for CNC machining, turning, milling operations
  • Good Weldability: Can be welded using TIG, MIG with proper techniques and filler materials
  • Superior Corrosion Resistance: Natural oxide layer provides protection in most environments
  • T6 Heat Treatment: Solution heat treated at 530°C + aging at 175°C = maximum strength
  • Formability: Fair to good formability, especially in T4 or O temper
  • Surface Treatment: Excellent response to anodizing - can achieve various colors
Best Applications for 6061-T6:
  • CNC Machined Components: Precision parts, enclosures, brackets, housings, fittings
  • Structural Applications: Frames, support structures, trusses, beams (where weight is critical)
  • Automotive Parts: Chassis components, drive shafts, suspension parts, wheels
  • Marine Applications: Boat fittings, marine hardware, yacht components, deck fittings
  • Electrical Components: Bus bars, connectors, electrical enclosures, heat sinks
  • Consumer Products: Bicycle frames, camera equipment, sporting goods, furniture
  • Aerospace (Non-Critical): Interior components, non-structural parts, ground support equipment
  • Food Processing: FDA compliant for food contact applications
Machining 6061-T6 - Best Practices:
  • Cutting Speed: 300-400 SFM (surface feet per minute) for HSS, 800-1000 SFM for carbide
  • Tool Material: Carbide inserts preferred for production, HSS acceptable for small runs
  • Coolant: Flood coolant or mist recommended, can run dry for light cuts
  • Surface Finish: Excellent surface finish achievable (Ra 0.4-0.8 µm typical)
  • Chip Control: Chips break easily - minimal chip evacuation issues
  • Threading: Excellent for tapping and threading operations
Why Choose 6061-T6? If you need a general-purpose aluminum bar and aren't sure which alloy to select, 6061-T6 is the safest choice. It offers the best balance of strength, machinability, weldability, corrosion resistance, and cost. Approximately 60% of all aluminum round bar usage is 6061-T6.
6061 vs 6082 - What's the Difference?

6082 is the European equivalent to 6061, with slightly different chemistry. 6082 has marginally higher strength and is preferred in European standards. For North American and Asian markets, 6061 is standard. Both are interchangeable for most applications.

The Strongest Aluminum Alloy for Commercial Use

Designation: 7075-T6 (UNS A97075, EN AW-7075)
Primary Alloying Element: Zinc (5.1-6.1%) + Magnesium (2.1-2.9%) + Copper (1.2-2.0%)
Strength Level: Ultra-high strength (572 MPa tensile, 505 MPa yield) - Comparable to many steels!

Why 7075-T6 is Special:
  • Maximum Strength: Highest strength-to-weight ratio of any commercial aluminum alloy
  • Fatigue Resistance: Excellent fatigue properties for cyclic loading applications
  • Hardness: 150 HB - hard enough for wear-resistant applications
  • Machinability: 70% - requires carbide tooling and proper cutting parameters
  • Weight Savings: Provides steel-like strength at 1/3 the weight
Critical Applications for 7075-T6:
  • Aerospace Structures: Aircraft wing spars, fuselage frames, landing gear components
  • High-Performance Automotive: Racing car components, drive shafts, suspension parts
  • Defense: Military aircraft, missile components, armor plating
  • Sporting Equipment: Rock climbing equipment, bicycle components, ski poles
  • Precision Tooling: Mold plates, jigs, fixtures requiring high strength and dimensional stability
  • High-Stress Components: Any application requiring maximum strength with minimum weight
Important Limitations:
  • ✗ NOT Weldable: Fusion welding causes severe loss of strength in heat-affected zone
  • ✗ Lower Corrosion Resistance: Requires protective coating or anodizing in corrosive environments
  • ✗ Not for Marine Use: Susceptible to stress corrosion cracking in saltwater
  • ✗ Expensive: 50-100% more expensive than 6061 due to copper and zinc content
  • ✗ Limited Formability: Difficult to bend or form - best machined from solid
Machining 7075-T6:

Requires carbide tooling due to high strength. Use slower speeds (600-800 SFM) and heavier feeds than 6061. Excellent surface finish achievable. Material tends to work-harden - avoid rubbing. Use plenty of coolant.

When to Choose 7075-T6: Only specify 7075-T6 when maximum strength is absolutely required and the additional cost is justified. For general applications, 6061-T6 provides adequate strength at much lower cost. Use 7075 for critical stress-bearing components in aerospace, defense, and high-performance applications.
Surface Treatment for 7075:

Always apply protective coating: Type III hard anodizing (most common), chromate conversion coating (Alodine), or primer + paint system. Without protection, 7075 will corrode in outdoor or marine environments.

Copper-Bearing High-Strength Alloy

Designation: 2014-T6 (UNS A92014, EN AW-2014)
Primary Alloying Element: Copper (3.9-5.0%) + Magnesium (0.20-0.8%)
Strength Level: High strength (485 MPa tensile, 415 MPa yield)

Key Characteristics:
  • High Strength: Excellent strength for aerospace applications (between 6061 and 7075)
  • Forgeability: Excellent hot forging characteristics - used for complex forgings
  • Machinability: Good to fair - requires proper tooling
  • Toughness: Better fracture toughness than 7075
Primary Applications:
  • Aerospace Forgings: Aircraft structural forgings, bulkheads, wing ribs
  • Truck Frames: Heavy-duty truck wheels and frames
  • Reciprocating Components: Pistons, connecting rods (engines, compressors)
  • High-Stress Parts: Components requiring high strength in elevated temperatures (up to 200°C)
Limitations:
  • Fair corrosion resistance - requires protective coating
  • Not recommended for welding (significant strength loss)
  • Not suitable for marine or corrosive environments without protection

When to Choose 2014-T6: Select for aerospace applications requiring high strength and fracture toughness, especially for forged components. Use when strength requirements exceed 6061 but extreme strength of 7075 isn't needed. Common in aircraft structural components and heavy-duty truck applications.

The Best Aluminum for Saltwater Applications

Designation: 5083 (UNS A95083, EN AW-5083)
Primary Alloying Element: Magnesium (4.0-4.9%)
Temper: H111 (slightly strain hardened) or H321 (strain hardened & stabilized)
Strength Level: Medium-high strength (305 MPa tensile minimum)

Why 5083 for Marine Applications:
  • Exceptional Seawater Resistance: Best corrosion resistance of all aluminum alloys in marine environments
  • Excellent Weldability: Can be welded without loss of corrosion resistance (use 5356 filler)
  • No Heat Treatment: Non-heat treatable - strength from work hardening and alloying
  • Cryogenic Properties: Retains toughness at very low temperatures (-196°C)
  • Good Formability: Can be formed, bent, rolled in H111 temper
Marine & Industrial Applications:
  • Shipbuilding: Ship hulls, superstructures, deck plates, bulkheads
  • Marine Components: Boat fittings, propeller shafts, marine railings, pontoons
  • Offshore Structures: Oil rig components, offshore platform structures
  • Cryogenic Tanks: LNG storage tanks, liquid nitrogen vessels, cryogenic piping
  • Pressure Vessels: Chemical storage tanks, process equipment
  • Architectural: Coastal building facades, bridges in marine environments
Advantages Over Other Alloys:
  • ✓ 10x better corrosion resistance than 6061 in saltwater
  • ✓ Weldable without strength loss (unlike 2014/7075)
  • ✓ Maintains properties in subzero temperatures
  • ✓ No stress corrosion cracking in marine environments
When to Choose 5083: Mandatory choice for any application involving saltwater exposure or marine environments. Also preferred for cryogenic applications and pressure vessels. While 6061 can be used in freshwater, 5083 is essential for seawater to prevent corrosion and ensure long-term reliability.
Machining Note:

5083 has lower machinability than 6061 (60% vs 90%) due to work hardening tendency. Use sharp tools, positive rake angles, and adequate coolant. Surface finish may not be as smooth as 6061 but is acceptable for marine applications.

Designation: 6082-T6 (EN AW-6082, AlSi1MgMn)
Relationship to 6061: Very similar chemistry and properties - often used interchangeably
Strength Level: Medium-high strength (295-310 MPa tensile, 260-310 MPa yield)
