Why Hexagonal Shape for CNC Machining?

Brass hex rods are specifically designed for automatic screw machines and CNC turning operations. The hexagonal shape provides three critical advantages:

  • Anti-Rotation Feature: The six flat sides prevent rotation in collets and chuck jaws, ensuring positive grip without marring the workpiece. Critical for high-speed automatic screw machines where slippage would cause dimensional errors or tool breakage.
  • Flat Loading Surface: Flat sides allow easy loading into bar feeders and magazine loaders. Round bars can roll, causing feed issues, but hex bars stack and feed reliably.
  • Less Material Waste: When machining round parts, less material needs to be removed from hex stock compared to round stock of equivalent diameter. The hex shape is closer to the final round geometry, reducing cycle time and scrap.
Understanding "Free-Cutting" Brass (CZ121/C360):

What is Free-Cutting Brass? Free-cutting brass contains 2-4% lead (Pb) which acts as a chip-breaker during machining. The lead creates natural weak points in the chip formation, causing chips to break into small, manageable pieces instead of long, stringy swarf. This results in:

  • 100% Machinability Rating: CZ121 (C360) is the benchmark material against which all other metals are compared for machinability. By definition, it has 100% rating.
  • Faster Cutting Speeds: Can run 50-100% faster than comparable non-leaded brasses without tool wear or quality issues.
  • Superior Surface Finish: Achieves mirror-like surface finish (0.4-0.8 Ra) directly from turning operations without secondary finishing.
  • Extended Tool Life: Reduced cutting forces and heat generation extend cutting tool life by 2-3 times.
  • Short Chip Formation: Chips break into 6-8mm segments that evacuate easily, preventing bird nesting and tool jamming in CNC machines.
  • Minimal Burrs: Clean cutting action produces minimal burrs, reducing deburring time and cost.

Hexagon Sizing - Understanding AF (Across Flats):

AF Measurement: Hexagons are measured "across flats" (AF) - the distance between two parallel flat sides. This is the critical dimension for tooling and collet sizing. A 10mm AF hex rod fits a 10mm collet perfectly.

Corner-to-Corner Distance: The diagonal distance across opposite corners is approximately 1.155 times the AF dimension. For a 10mm AF hex, the corner-to-corner distance is approximately 11.55mm. This is important when calculating the maximum round diameter that can be machined from a hex.

Inscribed Circle Diameter: The largest round part that can be machined from a hex has a diameter equal to the AF dimension. A 10mm AF hex can produce a 10mm diameter round part with zero waste on the flats.

Complete Brass Grades & Selection Guide for Hex Rods

Why CZ121/C360 is the Gold Standard for Machining

Designation: CZ121 (British Standard), C360 (US designation), CuZn39Pb3 (European)
Composition: 61% Copper, 36% Zinc, 3% Lead
Machinability Rating: 100% - The reference standard against which all other metals are measured

The Role of Lead in Free-Cutting Brass:

The 3% lead content is the secret to CZ121's exceptional machinability. Lead doesn't alloy with copper-zinc; instead, it exists as microscopic lead particles distributed throughout the brass matrix. During machining:

  • Lead particles create natural stress concentration points
  • Chips fracture at these points into short 6-8mm segments
  • Acts as a built-in lubricant, reducing friction and heat
  • Enables very high cutting speeds without tool wear
  • Produces excellent surface finish without chattering
Perfect Applications for CZ121 Hex Rods:
  • Automatic Screw Machine Parts: High-volume production of turned parts - watch components, clock parts, instrument components, precision fasteners
  • Valve Components: Valve stems, valve seats, valve bodies, actuator parts (non-potable applications only)
  • Plumbing Fittings (Non-Potable): Irrigation fittings, industrial fittings, compressed air fittings, gas fittings (where lead is permitted)
  • Electrical Components: Terminal blocks, connector bodies, switch components, electrical contacts
  • Fasteners: Special screws, bolts, nuts requiring precision threads and tight tolerances
  • Gears & Pinions: Small precision gears for instrumentation, clockwork mechanisms
  • Decorative Hardware: Door handles, cabinet hardware, furniture fittings, architectural trim
  • Automotive Parts: Carburetor components, fuel system fittings (non-emission critical)

Patina Development:

When exposed to outdoor atmosphere, C385 develops an attractive patina:

  • 0-6 months: Bright golden brass color darkens to brown
  • 6-12 months: Brown oxidation layer forms protective barrier
  • 1-3 years: Green patina begins to appear (copper carbonate)
  • 3+ years: Mature green patina fully developed - highly protective and aesthetically pleasing

This patina is self-healing and protects the underlying brass from further corrosion indefinitely.

The Best Brass for Saltwater Service

Composition: 60% Copper, 39% Zinc, 0.75-1.0% Tin
Key Feature: 1% tin addition dramatically improves seawater corrosion resistance
Machinability: 60% (lower than free-cutting brass but acceptable)

Why Tin Makes the Difference:

The 1% tin addition provides exceptional benefits:

  • Dezincification Resistance: Tin prevents selective leaching of zinc in seawater (dezincification) which would weaken standard brass
  • Biofouling Resistance: Tin provides natural antimicrobial properties reducing marine organism attachment
  • Pitting Resistance: Superior resistance to pitting corrosion in chloride-rich environments
  • Stress Corrosion Cracking: Better resistance to SCC in marine conditions

Machining Naval Brass:

Lower machinability than free-cutting brass but manageable:

  • Use carbide tooling for production runs
  • Cutting speeds: 100-200 m/min (slower than CZ121)
  • Continuous chip formation - need chip breaker or high-pressure coolant
