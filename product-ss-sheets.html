Complete SS Sheet Grades & Categories Guide

SS 304 / SS 304L Sheets

Most widely used grade - 18% Chromium, 8% Nickel composition. Excellent corrosion resistance, good formability, weldability. Applications: Kitchen equipment, food processing, dairy tanks, architectural trim, fasteners, automotive trim. 304L is low carbon variant for better weld performance.

SS 316 / SS 316L Sheets (Marine Grade)

Superior corrosion resistance - Contains 2-3% Molybdenum for enhanced resistance to chlorides and acids. Perfect for marine environments, coastal constructions, chemical processing, pharmaceutical equipment, surgical instruments, water treatment plants.

SS 202 Sheets (Budget-Friendly)

Cost-effective alternative to 304 - Lower nickel content replaced with manganese. Good for decorative applications, utensils, sinks, kitchen appliances, interior panels where high corrosion resistance is not critical.

SS 310S Sheets (High Temperature)

Heat resistant grade - 25% Chromium, 20% Nickel. Excellent oxidation resistance up to 1150°C. Used in furnace parts, heat treatment equipment, kilns, heat exchangers, boiler components.

SS 321 Sheets (Stabilized Grade)

Titanium stabilized - Prevents chromium carbide precipitation during welding. Ideal for applications involving 400-800°C temperatures. Used in aircraft exhaust systems, expansion joints, boiler shells.

SS 347 Sheets (Niobium Stabilized)

High temperature stability - Niobium addition prevents intergranular corrosion. Used in chemical processing, high-temperature applications, pressure vessels.

SS 904L Sheets (Super Austenitic)

Maximum corrosion resistance - High nickel and molybdenum content. Exceptional resistance to sulfuric acid, phosphoric acid, seawater. Used in desalination plants, chemical processing, pollution control equipment.

SS 430 Sheets

Most common ferritic grade - 17% Chromium, non-hardenable. Good corrosion resistance, magnetic properties. Applications: Automotive trim, kitchen appliances, dishwashers, indoor architectural applications.

SS 409 / SS 409L Sheets

Economical grade - 11% Chromium content. Used in automotive exhaust systems, mufflers, catalytic converters, heat exchangers.

SS 439 Sheets

Improved formability - Titanium stabilized ferritic grade. Better weldability than 430. Used in water heaters, automotive applications.

SS 410 Sheets

General purpose martensitic - Heat treatable, magnetic, moderate corrosion resistance. Applications: Cutlery, valve parts, pump shafts, bolts, bushings, mining machinery.

SS 420 Sheets

High carbon content - Excellent hardness after heat treatment. Used in surgical instruments, cutlery, scissors, razor blades, dental and surgical instruments.

SS 431 Sheets

High strength grade - Better corrosion resistance than 410. Used in aircraft fittings, nuts, bolts, valve parts.

SS 2205 Sheets (UNS S31803/S32205)

Most common duplex grade - Twice the yield strength of austenitic grades. Excellent resistance to stress corrosion cracking. Used in oil and gas, chemical tankers, heat exchangers, pressure vessels.

SS 2507 Sheets (Super Duplex)

Enhanced corrosion resistance - Higher chromium, molybdenum, and nitrogen content. Used in offshore platforms, seawater systems, chemical processing.

How to Choose the Right SS Sheet Grade?
  • For food contact: Use SS 304 or SS 316L (FDA approved)
  • For marine/coastal areas: Always use SS 316/316L with molybdenum
  • For high temperature (above 500°C): Choose SS 310S or SS 321
  • For budget projects: SS 202 or SS 430 for non-critical applications
  • For chemical plants: SS 904L or Duplex grades
  • For decorative/architectural: SS 304 with No.4 or Mirror finish
